  32. Termina Field
  33. The blazing mid afternoon sun still shone high in the blue skies when we reached the old, eroded southern gate. The guard had discarded his visor, his sweat-slicked face weary beneath the sunlit blaze. He slouched against the chalky white limestone wall, brow furrowed as he wiped at the heat with little urgency. Around him, the southern commons simmered in slow decay...unkempt locals sprawled in the patchwork shade cast by leaning shanty roofs, waving scraps of paper or warped tin lids to stir the still air. Barefoot children darted between crumbling alleyways, their laughter sharp against the dusty silence, vanishing like mirages among weed-choked cracks in the pavement.
